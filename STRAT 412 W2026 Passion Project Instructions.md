**Objective**

The primary objective for the passion project is to practice utilizing one or more of the specific software platforms covered in the course (i.e. Python, SQL, and Tableau) to a passion or interest of your own. That may mean doing some sort of analysis for your business, side hustle, internship (Sandbox or APM), or particular personal hobby or interest (i.e., NBA, college basketball, skiing, etc.). 

**Individual vs. Group Structure**

This passion project can be completed as a group (2-4 individuals). The minimum number of expected platforms used in your project should equal the number of project members. Thus, a group of two will be expected to use two platforms, a group of three is expected to use three platforms, etc. You may also count the use of R from STRAT 402 as one of your platforms. The key criteria for group selection should be a demonstrated interest in the data that you choose to collect and analyze. If you choose to work in a group, you may do so with students in the other section.

**Problem Selection**

You will need to identify a business problem or set of interrelated strategic questions that can ideally be addressed using at least one of the new software platforms that we will learn in class this semester (Python, SQL, and Tableau). Which platform you utilize will largely depend on the question, data context, and type of analysis that you want to perform. For example, you might use Tableau if you are interested in creating a dashboard, Python if you extract online data for your business, and SQL for generating insights from a large database.

The question or problem can either be a real question currently faced by the focal company or organization or a fictitious, but nevertheless, a realistic problem facing the company or organization. If you create a fictitious question, you will need to provide support for why you think this question is a likely problem faced by your focal company or organization. 

To help you identify important strategic questions, I will provide several examples of passion projects from prior semesters.

Your final deliverables will be a set of slides that you will present to the class, the corresponding data and code used to produce your analysis and results. **Presentation Guidelines**

You (or you and your group members) will record your passion project presentation. Your presentation should be no more than 6 minutes in length. 

Generally, each slide deck should be roughly outlined as follows:

* First slide – Company Background & History (Situation)  
* Second slide – Managerial Problem (Complication)  
* Third slide – Question addressed (Question)  
* Fourth Slide – Solution (Answer)  
* Next few slides – Method/Analysis/Results (Assumptions and Supporting Data)  
* Final slide – Recommendations/Conclusions (Answer)

You will need to submit a link to the recording, the slide deck, and your supporting data files to Learning Suite by Wednesday, April 15th at 11:59 PM. It is important that you submit any corresponding data files (i.e. spreadsheets, code, etc.) and that they be organized and annotated so that anyone can follow your analysis. Please do not make us guess how you arrived at your numbers. We should be able to follow your analysis all the way from your raw data to your final recommendations.
